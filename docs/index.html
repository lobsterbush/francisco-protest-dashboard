European Protest &
Coercion Dashboard

The most detailed daily-resolution record of political contention and state repression in modern Europe. Prof. Ron Francisco (University of Kansas) spent decades assembling this remarkable dataset — 29 countries, 16 years, and 88,000 coded events from strikes and demonstrations to arrests and bombings.

What's inside

Four ways to explore the data

📈

Overview

Summary statistics, a full protest & repression timeline across all 29 countries, top countries by protest activity, and breakdowns of the most common action types and issue categories.

All 88K events
🗺️

Trends

Filter by country (one or many) and year range. See annual time series for protest days, repression days, protest rate, and more. Explore a scatter of protest vs. repression at the country-year level.

Interactive filters
🔍

Events

Search and browse all 88,000+ individual coded events by keyword, country, year, action type, or issue. Export any filtered set to CSV or Excel. Includes protest size, state actor counts, and event descriptions.

Searchable · Exportable
📊

Models

Run your own statistical models in-browser. Choose from OLS, logit, or Poisson estimators with country and year fixed effects. Independent variables include lagged protest/repression, GDP per capita, unemployment, and population from the World Bank.

OLS · Logit · Poisson · FE
